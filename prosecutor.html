Judicial Activity

Honorary Prosecutor

Judicial activity carried out at the Public Prosecutor's Office of Milan, integrating courtroom practice, criminal investigations and legal scholarship.

Institutional Role

Since 2017, Francesco Ernesto Alessi Longa has served as Honorary Prosecutor at the Public Prosecutor's Office of Milan, one of Italy's leading prosecutorial offices.

His judicial activity includes hearings, criminal proceedings, coordination with judicial police authorities and participation in prosecutorial functions under Italian criminal procedure.

Alongside courtroom activity, he contributes to legal education and professional training through lectures and seminars in criminal law, international criminal law and cybercrime.

Areas of Judicial Activity

Criminal Proceedings

Participation in criminal hearings and prosecutorial functions.

Economic Crime

Corporate offences, financial investigations and white-collar crime.

Cybercrime

Digital investigations, cyber offences and electronic evidence.

International Cooperation

International criminal law and judicial cooperation.

Legal Education

Training activities and seminars for legal professionals.

Judicial Ethics

Professional responsibility, institutional integrity and rule of law.

Judicial Philosophy

Judicial activity requires impartiality, institutional responsibility and continuous legal study. Practical courtroom experience strengthens academic research, while research contributes to a deeper understanding of criminal justice and procedural guarantees.

Academic Integration

The interaction between judicial practice and academic research constitutes a central element of his professional profile, particularly in the fields of criminal law, international law, sports law and legal innovation.
