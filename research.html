Research

Scientific activity focused on Criminal Law, International Law, Sports Law and interdisciplinary legal research.

OVERVIEW

Research Profile

My research integrates legal scholarship, judicial experience and sports governance through an interdisciplinary and evidence-based approach.

My scientific activity focuses on the interaction between legal theory, institutional practice and emerging global challenges. Research projects investigate criminal justice, international governance, sports law, safeguarding, artificial intelligence and cybercrime.

Particular attention is devoted to comparative legal systems, the evolution of Lex Sportiva, international human rights, evidence-based regulation and the relationship between law and technological innovation.

Current work also examines anti-doping regulation, sports integrity, governance models and international judicial cooperation through multidisciplinary methodologies.

METHODOLOGY

Research Methodology

Methodological approach adopted in legal and interdisciplinary research.

Research combines doctrinal legal analysis, comparative law, empirical evidence, policy evaluation and interdisciplinary methodologies. Particular attention is devoted to integrating legal scholarship with judicial practice and evidence-based decision making.

The methodological framework also includes comparative institutional analysis, systematic literature review and evaluation of governance models across legal and sporting systems.
