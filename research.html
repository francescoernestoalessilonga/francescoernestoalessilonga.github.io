Research

Research Areas

My research integrates legal practice, judicial experience and academic scholarship across criminal law, international law and sports governance.

Criminal Law

Economic crime, corporate criminal liability, criminal procedure, judicial cooperation and prosecution.

International Law

Human rights, international organizations, humanitarian law and comparative legal systems.

Sports Law

Lex Sportiva, Olympic governance, sports justice, safeguarding and dispute resolution.

Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence regulation, legal ethics, blockchain and emerging technologies.

Cybercrime

Digital investigations, cyber offences, electronic evidence and international cooperation.

Sport Science

Performance analysis, kinesiology, evidence-based practice and interdisciplinary research.

Current Research Projects

Sports Governance and Lex Sportiva

Comparative legal analysis of international sports governance and regulatory systems.

Artificial Intelligence and Criminal Justice

Impact of AI on criminal investigations, evidence and judicial decision-making.

International Criminal Cooperation

Cross-border prosecution, judicial cooperation and procedural guarantees.

Sports Integrity

Anti-doping, safeguarding, athlete protection and institutional accountability.

Research Philosophy

My research combines practical legal experience with academic methodology, aiming to produce evidence-based scholarship capable of supporting legal professionals, policymakers and international institutions. Particular attention is devoted to the interaction between national legal systems, international organizations and transnational regulatory frameworks.
