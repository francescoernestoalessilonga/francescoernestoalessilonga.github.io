OVERVIEW

Research Publications

This page presents a selection of scientific publications and research outputs in criminal law, international law, sports law and interdisciplinary legal studies.

The research activity combines doctrinal legal analysis, comparative law and interdisciplinary methodologies. Publications address topics including criminal justice, sports governance, anti-doping regulation, human rights, artificial intelligence, cybercrime and international legal systems.

Scientific work is developed through collaboration with universities, research centres and international scholars, with particular attention to evidence-based legal research and institutional innovation.

FUTURE RESEARCH

Current and Future Research Directions

Research activities continue to evolve through interdisciplinary collaborations and international academic partnerships.

Current projects investigate the interaction between criminal law, sports law, international governance and emerging technologies, with particular attention to artificial intelligence, safeguarding, anti-doping regulation and comparative legal systems.

Future research aims to strengthen international scientific cooperation while promoting evidence-based legal scholarship capable of supporting institutional decision-making and public policy.
