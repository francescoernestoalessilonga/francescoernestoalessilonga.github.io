Teaching Philosophy

Teaching is based on the integration of legal theory, judicial practice and academic research. The objective is to provide students and professionals with analytical tools capable of addressing complex legal issues through critical reasoning, comparative analysis and evidence-based methodology.

Main Teaching Areas

Criminal Law

Substantive criminal law, criminal procedure and prosecutorial practice.

International Law

Human rights, international institutions and comparative legal systems.

Sports Law

Lex Sportiva, sports governance, safeguarding and sports justice.

Research Methodology

Academic writing, peer review, publication ethics and scientific methodology.

Artificial Intelligence

Legal implications of AI, digital innovation and technological regulation.

Professional Ethics

Legal ethics, judicial ethics and institutional responsibility.

Educational Mission

The combination of legal practice, judicial activity and academic scholarship provides students with a multidisciplinary perspective capable of connecting legal doctrine with institutional practice and international research.
